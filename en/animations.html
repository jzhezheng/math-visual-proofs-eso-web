Algebraic Identities

Visual geometric proofs of fundamental algebraic formulas

Square of a Sum: (a + b)²

Geometric proof using area decomposition

Mathematical Concept

The identity (a + b)² = a² + 2ab + b² demonstrates how the area of a large square with side length (a + b) can be decomposed into smaller geometric components.

Geometric Proof Steps

  1. Construct a large square with side length (a + b)
  2. Divide into four regions: a² square, b² square, and two ab rectangles
  3. Show total area equals sum of all regions
  4. Algebraically: (a + b)² = a² + b² + ab + ab = a² + 2ab + b²
(a + b)² = a² + 2ab + b²

Square of a Difference: (a - b)²

Geometric proof with area subtraction

Mathematical Concept

The identity (a - b)² = a² - 2ab + b² shows how removing rectangles from a larger square and correcting for overlap results in the area of a smaller square.

Geometric Proof Steps

  1. Start with a large square of area a²
  2. Remove two rectangles, each of area ab
  3. Notice that area b² was subtracted twice
  4. Add back b² to correct for over-subtraction
  5. Remaining area equals (a - b)²
(a - b)² = a² - 2ab + b²

Product of Sum and Difference: (a + b)(a - b)

Geometric transformation proof

Mathematical Concept

The identity (a + b)(a - b) = a² - b² demonstrates that the product can be represented as the difference between areas and rearranged geometrically.

Geometric Proof Steps

  1. Start with square area a² minus square area b²
  2. Remaining area is a² - b²
  3. Rearrange L-shaped region into a rectangle
  4. Show rectangle has dimensions (a + b) × (a - b)
  5. Therefore, (a + b)(a - b) = a² - b²
(a + b)(a - b) = a² - b²

Triangle Area Formulas

Geometric proofs of area formulas in different scenarios

Triangle Area from Rectangle

Derivation from bounding rectangle area

Mathematical Concept

Any triangle's area is exactly half the area of its bounding rectangle, providing the fundamental geometric insight for the area formula.

Geometric Proof Steps

  1. Construct bounding rectangle around triangle
  2. Show triangle occupies half the rectangle's area
  3. Calculate rectangle area as base × height
  4. Divide by two for triangle area
  5. Generalize to all triangle types
A = ½ × b × h

Triangle Area: Interior Altitude

When the altitude lies inside the triangle

Mathematical Concept

The area formula Area = ½ × base × height holds when the altitude falls within the triangle, decomposing it into two right triangles.

Geometric Proof Steps

  1. Draw altitude from vertex to base
  2. Divide triangle into two right triangles
  3. Calculate area of each right triangle
  4. Sum the areas to get total area
  5. Simplify to ½ × base × height
A = ½ × b × h

Triangle Area: Exterior Altitude

When the altitude falls outside the triangle

Mathematical Concept

The area formula Area = ½ × base × height remains valid even when the altitude lies outside the triangle, using area subtraction.

Geometric Proof Steps

  1. Extend the base and draw exterior altitude
  2. Form a larger triangle containing the original
  3. Calculate area as difference of two right triangles
  4. Simplify the expression
  5. Show result equals ½ × base × height
A = ½ × b × h

Geometric proof of a² + b² = c² through area equivalence

Pythagorean Theorem: a² + b² = c²

Area-based geometric proof with dynamic demonstration

Mathematical Concept

The Pythagorean Theorem states that in a right triangle, the square of the hypotenuse equals the sum of the squares of the other two sides: a² + b² = c².

Geometric Proof Steps

  1. Construct a square with side length (a + b)
  2. Arrange four congruent right triangles inside
  3. Show the remaining area forms a square with side length c
  4. Calculate total area in two different ways
  5. Equate the expressions to derive a² + b² = c²
a² + b² = c²

Quadratic Formula Derivation

Geometric and algebraic proof of the solution formula

Mathematical Concept

The quadratic formula provides the solutions to any quadratic equation: x = [-b ± √(b² - 4ac)] / 2a, derived geometrically through completing the square.

Geometric Proof Steps

  1. Represent x² as a square area
  2. Represent bx as rectangular areas
  3. Complete the square geometrically
  4. Balance the equation algebraically
  5. Solve for x to derive the formula
x = [-b ± √(b² - 4ac)] / 2a
